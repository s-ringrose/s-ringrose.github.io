About Me

I am a graduate of 2020 with a Bachelor's degree in Geography, supplemented by completion of the Teach First Development Program. Over the span of three years, I dedicated myself to teaching Mathematics in comprehensive secondary schools. During this period, I developed an interest in the analytical aspects of education, particularly in handling data through Excel spreadsheets.

Motivated by this interest, I started a coding traineeship, where I was introduced to the world of programming. I completed modules in SQL and Python and they reminded me of the data analysis I completed durning my Geography degree. Eager to expand my capabilities, I pursued independent projects centered around data analysis using SQL, Python and Excel.

Today, I possess a proficient command of SQL alongside Python, Excel, Power BI, and Tableau. My experience in these tools allows me to approach data-driven challenges with confidence and precision, leveraging insights to drive informed decision-making.

House Prices 2023: Data Cleaning and Exploration

  • Data Cleaning

    I downloaded the data set from the gov.uk. I cleaned the data, removed any missing data, standardised the dates, concatenated the address data, and split up the post codes so I could compare different areas. I ensured the data was easier to understand by changing abreviations to their original values.

  • Data Exploration

    I was particularly interested in looking at the average prices per building type and comparing the number of houses I could get around my postcode. I looked at house prices <= £250,000 and <= £450,000 to compare the number and percentage of homes eligable with a Helpt to Buy compared to a LISA. I looked at the number and percentage of freehold and leasehld houses sorted by building type. I looked at seasonal trends by comparing the difference in average sale prices scross the seasons.

  • Data Presentation

    I created a Power BI dashboard to show the exploration of data.
